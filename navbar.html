'Gold' has been regarded as special since ancient times.

Gold, with its stable properties and long-lasting brilliance, has been regarded as a 'special metal' since ancient times. For example, it was already used as an 'ornament' around 6000-5000 BC. Glittering jewellery must have been a special item, worn only by people of high status. We also know that gold coins were used in many parts of the world from around 700-600 BC. Gold was also used as currency. Now and in the past, gold was treated as 'highly valuable' because it was not easy to obtain and because of its beautiful, ever-luminous properties. Even today, 'gold' is treated as a special metal. Many people hold it as a "safe asset", as an investment object that also has value on its own.
